Conferenza Episcoplae Italiana: Project Summary

Salem Social Service Society (SSSS) has planned to promote four qualities employable skill development courses for 1160 poor Dalit women in 40 village panchayats in four of the least developed blocks in Salem District. A considerable section of them belong to Dalit Hindus and Catholic communities. Due to the prevailing social stratification, the Dalit communities experience the brunt of socio-economic deprivation and discrimination. The young women have fewer options to find work because they do not possess the necessary trade skills.

The overall objectives of this project are:

  1. To enable dalit young women above 18 years in Salem Districts to overcome poverty and hunger and deliver from traditional slavery employment system at local, and
  2. To support Marginalized Dalit women and individuals for livelihood advancement and enterprise â€“SMILE.

This project has been planned to impart regular theory and practical classes at the Community Skill Development Centre [SKILL HUB] in each block. Day scholar programmes will replace residential programmes for the four marketable skill training programmes:

  • Dairy farmer course (8 days for a batch)
  • Coir making (6 months for a batch)
  • Junior software developer course (3 months for a batch)
  • Self-employed tailoring and embroidery course (6 months for a batch)

After the training, they will be given course completion certificates and we will facilitate them to get placement in viable employment. The Dalit youth women These trade courses have better job market opportunities. The benevolent support of BCI will serve as a token to launch and maintain Block Level Community Skill Development Center (SKILL HUB) operations. It has a significant potential impact in our society as well as Diocese. These Skill Hub Centers will address the issue of unemployment and serve as a beacon of hope for thousands of young people in our diocese. The young Dalit women will have improved employment opportunities and greater trade skills. They will have developed the self-assurance necessary to live a happy and successful life. After that, they will have been in a better economic situation to be able to raise their level of living because they will have gained employment.

The Project directly supports 600 youth from poor economic background from the villages around Salem and Namakkal, the headquarters of the district. After the training, the trainees will place in various companies, industries, mills etc., and established micro enterprise to enhance their employability as well livelihood security.

Location of the Project:

This Project is implemented in underdeveloped 2 blocks Attur and Gangavalli with socially and economically excluded dalits in Salem District of Tamilnadu.

Target Group

880 poor dalit women are the target group. They are standing far from mainstream of development.
